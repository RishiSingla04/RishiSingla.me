Auction process

Clock Auctions (CA) and Combinatorial Clock Auctions (CCA) are structured, multi-stage bidding processes designed to efficiently allocate resources such as spectrum licenses. The following outlines the complete auction process from initial consultation to license issuance.

ISED manages spectrum to maximize economic and social benefits for Canadians. When the demand for spectrum is not expected to exceed the supply, ISED generally uses a first come, first served licensing process to award spectrum licences. When the demand for spectrum is expected to exceed supply, a competitive licensing process, such as an auction, is generally used.

1. Consultations on the Proposed Policy and Licensing Procedures

A formal notice is published in the Canada Gazette announcing the availability of a consultation paper addressing all aspects of the upcoming auction. This document outlines the proposed spectrum policy, auction design, and licensing procedures and rules. The consultation process provides interested stakeholders with the opportunity to review and comment on these proposals before the auction framework is finalized.

2. Comment period

Formal timeframe during which interested parties can submit written comments on the proposed auction design and rules. All stakeholders including potential bidders, industry associations, and public interest groups may participate.

3. Reply comment period

Secondary period allowing stakeholders to respond to comments submitted during the initial comment period. This provides opportunity for addressing concerns or refining proposals based on feedback. The comments are later made available on the ISED website.

4. Publication of the licensing framework/decision

Release of the final auction rules, procedures, and licensing conditions. This document incorporates relevant feedback from the consultation process and includes detailed information on eligibility requirements, auction format, bidding rules, and post-auction obligations.

5. Deadline for the receipt of applications

Submission period when prospective participants file their completed application forms with required documentation, including beneficial ownership information and financial deposits. The specific application requirements are detailed in the auction's consultation documents and the published licensing framework.

6. Publication of the list of applicants, beneficial ownership and affiliate associate information

Shortly after the application submission deadline, ISED publishes a comprehensive list of all applicants on its website. This disclosure includes information about beneficial ownership and affiliations between applicants, but does not indicate qualification status for auction participation.

7. Publication of the list of qualified bidders

ISED publishes the official list of qualified bidders on its website, specifying the licenses each bidder is eligible to bid on and their initial eligibility points for the auction.

8. Bidder information sessions

Educational session for qualified bidders providing detailed explanation of auction rules, software platforms, and bidding procedures, with opportunity for bidders to ask questions about the auction process.

12. Publication of provisional licence winners

Official announcement of auction results, including disclosure of winning bidders, licenses won, and final prices, with a summary of the auction process.

13. Initial payment deadline

Date by which winners must submit their first payment installment, typically a percentage of the total winning bid amount. Failure to meet this deadline may result in forfeiture of winning bids.

14. Final payment deadline

Due date for the remaining balance of winning bids, which may occur in multiple stages for high-value auctions and must be completed before licenses can be issued.

15. Issuance of licences

Formal granting of licenses to winning bidders who have fulfilled all payment obligations. This marks the conclusion of the auction process.
